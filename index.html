VISUALIZING: Census 2011 data

The Census 2011 was a massive undertaking conducted by the Government of India to gather comprehensive demographic and socio-economic data about the country's population. It was the 15th National Census of India and provided crucial insights into the changing dynamics of the nation.

  • Objective: The primary objective of the Census 2011 was to collect data on various parameters such as population size, density, growth rate, literacy, education, occupation, and housing conditions. This information helps in policy formulation, planning, and resource allocation at both national and regional levels.
  • Population Size: The Census 2011 revealed that the total population of India had crossed the 1.2 billion mark, making it the second most populous country in the world after China. It provided detailed population figures for each state, district, and village in the country.
  • Demographic Data: The Census 2011 collected demographic data on age, gender, marital status, fertility rates, and population distribution across rural and urban areas. It helped in analyzing population trends, identifying age cohorts, and understanding the gender ratio in different regions.
